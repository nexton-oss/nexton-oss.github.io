Clear position on trustworthy AI systems

Why we publish

To show real engineering standards, share reusable building blocks and make part of our work legible to the people who may one day build with us.

How we publish

Selectively, after review and usage, with the least possible client-specific material, the least possible sensitive information and the least possible operational risk.

What we optimize for

Useful assets, honest trade-offs, local-first AI when relevant, and systems that can be understood, reused and improved over time.

NextEco

Make the cost of running software explicit inside the repository itself.

A framework for estimating and documenting software cost across money, time, energy and carbon. It is designed for teams that want honest trade-offs, reproducible estimates and engineering accountability.

Why it matters

Software is never free once it runs in the real world. NextEco makes cost, energy and carbon legible inside the repository itself so teams can reason about trade-offs early.

  • Makes engineering accountability explicit across money, time, energy and carbon.
  • Fits a European conversation on sobriety, sovereignty and honest trade-offs.
  • Turns architecture choices into measurable and reviewable decisions.

Web Browser LLM RAG

Run an LLM and PDF RAG fully locally in the browser.

A browser-native local AI application that keeps inference and retrieval on the client side. It demonstrates private, CPU-friendly, serverless interaction with documents directly in the browser.

Why it matters

Not every AI workflow needs a server, a GPU bill or a data transfer outside the browser. This project explores private, local-first interaction with documents directly on the client side.

  • Runs local LLM inference and PDF RAG inside the browser.
  • Demonstrates privacy-preserving, CPU-friendly and serverless document interaction.
  • A strong signal for local-first AI and practical sovereignty.

md2star

Write in Markdown, deliver in Word and PowerPoint.

A practical toolchain for turning Markdown into polished office deliverables. It reduces formatting friction and keeps technical writing close to the source while producing outputs that organizations actually use.

Why it matters

Technical writing loses value when it becomes painful to deliver in the formats companies actually use. md2star keeps the source of truth close to Markdown while still producing office-ready outputs.

  • Bridges Markdown and office deliverables without forcing teams to rewrite content.
  • Makes technical documentation easier to maintain and easier to ship.
  • Useful for consulting, internal knowledge and reproducible reporting workflows.

Trust by design, not by claim.

NEXTON publishes selected open-source assets to demonstrate engineering standards, share reusable building blocks and promote a responsible approach to AI systems.

We prefer trust by design over trust by claim or certification alone. In practice, that means exposing the least possible client-specific material, the least possible sensitive information and the least possible operational risk.

Every repository is deliberately selected, clearly owned and published only after review and usage when it reflects the engineering standards NEXTON chooses to stand behind.
