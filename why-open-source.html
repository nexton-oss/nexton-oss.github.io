Why NEXTON publishes open source

Open source is the viable infrastructure of modern AI. We publish selected engineering assets because we would rather build than merely consume.

Why open source?

Open source is not a trend. It is the viable infrastructure of modern artificial intelligence.

The most important AI systems, from foundational libraries to advanced models, depend on open dynamics. Not by ideology, but by necessity. Complex systems require transparency, iteration and collective scrutiny.

A technical civilization often needs a resource that is abundant, accessible and shareable. For software, and now for AI, that resource is the commons.

At NEXTON, we see open source as a technical necessity, a strategic lever and a cultural commitment.

Why in France?

France and Europe do not lack talent, scientific depth or engineering rigor.

But too often the same pattern repeats: technologies are conceived elsewhere, industrialized elsewhere and then adopted in Europe.

In that model, Europe risks becoming a space of consumption and regulation rather than production, pushed toward the outskirts of the digital world.

We reject that trajectory. AI changes the economics of software production. With stronger tools, more automation and augmented engineers, it becomes possible to produce more locally with smaller and more expert teams.
